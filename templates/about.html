Introduction

Over the past decades, a continuous evolution related to cancer research has been performed. Scientists applied different methods, such as screening in early stage, in order to find types of cancer before they cause symptoms. Moreover, they have developed new strategies for the early prediction of cancer treatment outcome. With the advent of new technologies in the field of medicine, large amounts of cancer data have been collected and are available to the medical research community. However, the accurate prediction of a disease outcome is one of the most interesting and challenging tasks for physicians. As a result, ML methods have become a popular tool for medical researchers. These techniques can discover and identify patterns and relationships between them, from complex datasets, while they are able to effectively predict future outcomes of a cancer.

Who is it for?

It is based on the report of checkups either the doctor or a normal user can check their status of the presence of cancer. As this is user friendly for both doctors and patients they can get the result in the easiest way.

How Predict works

The estimates that Predict produces are based on scientific studies that have been conducted into how effective breast cancer treatments. We know from studies involving many thousands of women that the benefit from treatment is affected by the size and type of the cancer at diagnosis, Predict has been tested to make sure that the estimates it produces are as accurate as possible. We have used machine learning algorithm which suits as a best model to the data which is obtained from previous breast cancer results. As our model is trained on huge amount of data and a good algorithm it able to produce an accuracy rate of 96.1%. *These results may not be 100% sure but the model predicts good results upto 96%.
